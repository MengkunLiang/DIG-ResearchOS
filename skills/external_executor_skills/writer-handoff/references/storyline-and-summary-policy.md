# Storyline and Summary Policy

Summaries help T7 and later writers navigate evidence. They are not evidence themselves.

## Method summary

Describe only realized modules, actual algorithm flow, implemented losses, and known delta from method intent. Distinguish implemented from empirically supported.

## Result summary

Use structured result IDs and source refs. Separate main formal candidates, ablations, diagnostics, robustness/efficiency, failures, and unavailable comparisons. Do not manually transcribe unsupported numbers.

## Recommended storyline update

Use `status=pre_audit_suggestion`. It may propose emphasis, de-emphasis, narrowing, or a limitations-first narrative, but it cannot create final claims or contribution statements. It must cite diagnosis/attribution/decision references and include caveats.

## Language

Avoid `proves`, `establishes`, `demonstrates conclusively`, `SOTA`, `paper-ready`, and similar closure language. Prefer `candidate evidence suggests`, `under the tested protocol`, `pending T7 audit`, and `not supported outside these settings`.
