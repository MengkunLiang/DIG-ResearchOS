# Academic Writing Policy

## Prose

Write accurate, compact academic prose. Organize related facts into complete paragraphs with explicit causal, contrastive, progressive, or limiting relations. Do not create rhythm with repeated fragments, em dashes, excessive colons, slogans, or mechanically parallel short sentences.

Avoid empty evaluations such as “important,” “significant,” “clear,” or “superior” unless the sentence gives the exact result and valid inferential basis. Do not repeat conclusions without adding information.

Introduce the research question before the method, the method before the experiment design, and the design before interpreting results. Explain a technical term when it first appears. A concrete example may clarify a concept but cannot replace its definition or evidence.

## Structure

Use the eight required major sections. Do not create many micro-headings or paragraph-level labels. Use tables for repeated audit fields and connected prose for motivation, implementation rationale, result interpretation, and limitations.

## Quantitative statements

Every number must come from a structured final table or a path-bound raw result. State dataset, split or condition, comparator, aggregation, uncertainty, seed count, metric direction, statistical-test status, and source paths when available. Do not calculate or report a favorable percentage selectively.

## Formulae

Include a formula only when it appears in the realized method or can be verified from final code/specification. Define each symbol at first use and explain what the equation represents, why the form is needed, and how it relates to the research question and experiments.

## Citations

Transfer only verified executor additions. Confirm existence, authors, year, venue, identifier, access level, and sentence-level support. Venue prestige never substitutes for relevance. Do not generalize a conditional finding or cite a paper merely because its topic is similar.

## Claim authority

Do not use `proves`, `accepted`, `paper-ready`, `state-of-the-art`, `universally superior`, or equivalent closure language. Bound every interpretation to tested evidence. Final Claim decisions belong to T8.
